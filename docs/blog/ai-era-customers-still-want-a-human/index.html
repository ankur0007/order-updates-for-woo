In the AI era, your customers still want a human

July 2026 · ~4 min read

Everyone wants to sell faster and make more profit. That’s fair — it’s a business. But very few stores put the same energy into what happens after the sale. And that’s where they quietly lose people.

Right now everyone is adding AI to everything. And look, AI is genuinely useful. It sorts messages, drafts replies, and answers the easy stuff in seconds. I use it too. If it saves your team time, use it.

But here is the part people forget.

When something goes wrong, buyers want a person

Think about the last time you had a problem with an order. A wrong item. A refund. A parcel that never showed up. Did you want a chatbot asking you to “rephrase your question”? Or did you want a real person who could open your order and actually help?

Your customers feel the same. Most of them are not technical. They don’t care about your tools. They just want to feel heard, and to know a human is on the other side sorting it out.

A buyer who feels heard comes back and buys again. A buyer stuck in a bot loop leaves — and tells other people why.

The problem isn’t AI. It’s where support lives.

For most WooCommerce stores, support is a mess. The question comes by email. The order is in WooCommerce. The team copies bits between the two all day. Things get lost. The same question gets asked three times because nobody knows what was already answered.

So owners do one of two things: they skip proper support, or they buy a heavy paid help desk that is far more than a small store needs.

A simpler way: keep the human, right on the order

This is exactly why I built Order Updates for WooCommerce. It puts a simple conversation right on the order — your team on one side, the customer on the other, and the full history in one place. No separate inbox. No copying between tools.

You can still let AI help you draft a reply. But the person reading it can see the order, the past messages, and the customer — so the answer is real, not canned. And it’s free.

Sell fast. Chase profit. That’s all good. Just keep a real human at the back for the moments that matter. It builds trust, it brings people back, and — honestly — it costs a lot less than you think.
